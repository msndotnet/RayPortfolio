Bike Bolt

Bike Bolt is a concept app that aims to improve the transaction between the cyclist and the local bike shops. This project involves team building, concept design, and critical analytic research.

Ideation

Early iteration, my team and I were tasked to design a prototype app that should solve people’s problems. We begin to focus the app’s direction towards helping the needs of cyclists in the Vancouver area. After our brainstorm stage, we eventually settled with the theme of bike repairs.

I took the role of product manager for the team and settled the team’s goal, which is to make the app help cyclists in any level of experience that may beconcerned or interested in the state of their bicycle.

01

User Study

In our second stage of development, we wanted to focus on the meaning of accessible user experience for cyclists. I began my user research by doing both quantitative and qualitative research, such as personal interviews with general online surveys. Then I proceed to evaluate the data I was given to look for design possibilities that can appeal to the issues presented in the data

The data revealed that cyclists in Vancouver could sometimes experience inconsistency when it comes to bike repairs; some bike shops in Vancouver was known for being more offline. We eventually settled with four main core features starting with purchasing new bike parts, access to location services, a bike profile/ virtual garage, and a bike part history.

02

Final Prototype

There were several challenges met along the way, such as information presentation, functional or esthetic design decisions, colour scheme. I tasked myself to complete the feature to buy bike parts and focus on ensuring the potential user does not get confused throughout the purchasing process.

I want the app was to be powerful, dynamic, modern and easy to use. The result was that the final interface was clean, comfortable to follow, and the new feedback that was given was overall positive.
