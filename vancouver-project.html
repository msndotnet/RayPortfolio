Vancouver Eco Footprint 2020

Web Development Project

Vancouver Eco Footprint 2020 is a front-end web development project that aims to use present and educate audience how Vancouver aims to be the cleanest city in the world by 2020.

Home Page Screenshot

Context

The team consists of 3 members, intending to arrange the information given by the Vancouver city government and apply it on a website to educated online visitors. My role is to develop 2 of the web pages and design an offline physical promotion property to attract new users to our website.

Time Frame

Academic assignment starts at the beginning of January 2018 and ended in April. The team would work on the assignment for 6 hours per week.

  • Brainstorm
  • Website page journey setup
  • Graybox
  • HTML & CSS
  • Offline property design
  • Presentation + Hubbub10 participation

Objective

Vancouver’s road to 2020 is a academic project that aims to teach individuals about Vancouver’s projected future on reducing its ecological footprint by 2020. The team created a website and a prototye compass card concept. With only pie charts and scientific research documents, this project aims to simplify the data that was given and to promote how Vancouver is aiming to become the greenest city in the world.

The city of Vancouver provided source materials such as 'Ecocity Footprint Overview' and 'Ecological Footprint Assessment' data graphics for the purpose of the course. Our task as desingers is to figure out where and how do we develop a website and communite to a broad audience with the following data we recieved.

Information Design

Decision Process

Information display such as the pie charts and line graphs needs to be represented differently in a way that visitors will not look away immediately. Based on our readings, academically approved articles, and along with our lecture notes, there are many studies that reveals why an average person has a habit to not view pic charts or bar graphs to retain information.

Statistical Graphics

I proposed a more satisfying alternative, such as groups of gauges that works similar to a vehicle odometer to represent information. Some challenges include technical language and word count.

Wireframe

Menus & Content

We started with a simple wireframe to get a general feeling where the content should go. There were several design choices that were made whether or not to have the information displayed right away on the home page. We believed that a general outline with several introduction inforomation is suffificent. There were also different design iteration how the navigation menu should operate. We eventually laid out all the webpages instead of a dropdown navbar style.

Offline Physical Prototype

Development process

For the offline approach of the project, the team settled with the idea of limited-edition compass cards can incentives users to use our website. I decided to go with 4 different themes, foods, transportation, recycling, and renewable energy. When the customer purchases a limited-edition compass card, it will display facts about average ecological footprint by people living in Vancouver. On the backside of the card will provide the customer an incentives code whichthey can redeem it on the website.

My team and I were able to brainstorm and produce different design solutions.
